CO-OPERATIVE EDUCATION PROGRAM

The Co-operat ive Education Program takes you beyond the classroom as it prepares you for paid work experience in a relevant field .

Co-op education combines in-class learning with the opportunity to gain valuable paid employment in your chosen field. Co-op students complete a non-credit course in pre-employment skills, workshops and networking opportunities as well as meet with the Co-op Coordinator to discuss their career goals. You’ll be assisted in applying for a co-op work term from positions posted by prospective employers. Although there is no guarantee of a job, the program has been successful in attracting interesting companies looking to hire a co-op student.

The University of Winnipeg's Co-operative Education Program is open to students from the Departments of Applied Computer Science, Business, and Economics. Students from other departments are welcome to apply and will be reviewed on a case by case analysis. Work terms are typically four months in duration and may begin in January, May or September. You will work in a professional environment, using the knowledge and skills that you learned in the classroom. This work experience will add to your resume as well as make you aware of different career options within your field. If you choose the for-credit option, you can also earn three general elective credit hours towards your degree.

When you complete a work term through the University’s Co-op Program, it appears on your transcript, whether you choose the for-credit option or the not-for-credit option. When you complete three work terms, you will receive a Co-operative Education Designation on your degree when you graduate.

BENEFITS OF JOINING THE CO-OPERATIVE EDUCATION PROGRAM

Set yourself apart. Co-op work terms show employers that you have the requisite academic knowledge and training, and that you can apply it effectively in the workplace.

Gain real life work experience and an opportunity to learn in an enriching environment while building your professional networks.

Earn while you learn. In a Co-op work term, you can apply the latest theories and approaches learned in the classroom whie earning an income working in your field.
